# IJF Editorial Notes

## Reproducibility
- Ensure each figure and table can be regenerated from versioned code.
- Keep dataset provenance explicit and auditable.
- Make train-only preprocessing decisions visible in code and manuscript.
- Report exact rolling-origin setup, horizon definitions, and baseline construction.

## Overlap disclosure
- Prepare a manuscript-level overlap memo before submission.
- Disclose any dataset reuse, concept reuse, or figure lineage explicitly.
- Separate neutral background continuity from genuine contribution claims.

## Contribution legibility
- Keep the integrated protocol as the main contribution.
- Avoid overstating H*, Skill_VP, Ghost Skill, or LRS as standalone inventions.
- Make clear what is methodological, what is empirical, and what is diagnostic.

## Forecasting relevance
- Keep the framing operational and horizon-aware.
- Explain why persistence-relative evaluation matters for practice.
- Emphasize decision usefulness over leaderboard optics.

## Evaluation rigor
- Leakage-free rolling-origin only.
- Horizon-wise results must be preserved in tables and plots.
- Persistence baseline must be explicit everywhere.
- Diagnostic analysis must accompany any claims of improvement where variance attenuation is suspected.
